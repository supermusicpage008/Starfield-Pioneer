System Overview

The All Terrain Walking Explorer integrates the speed and efficiency of wheeled systems with the adaptability of legged locomotion.

A.T.W.E. represents a breakthrough in planetary robotics, designed to overcome the critical flaw of rover rollovers on extreme terrains. Our system prevents catastrophic mission failure and immense financial loss by merging the speed of wheels on flat ground with the superior stability and adaptability of legs on rough landscapes.

Prevents rollover and provides access to significantly more varied terrain

Equipped with ultrasonic and vision-based sensing systems for real-time terrain perception

Automatic obstacle avoidance for obstacles up to 10 cm, traversal up to 30 cm

Maximum speed of 100 cm/s on flat terrain with high-performance mobility on slopes

Core Innovations

Breakthrough technologies designed to overcome critical challenges in planetary exploration.

1. Wheel-Leg Moving Mode

Our robot features a land-adaptive mechanism that switches between efficient wheeled motion and stable legged locomotion, increasing obstacle clearance capability by 50% and enabling reliable traversal of slopes over 25° and obstacles exceeding 20 cm in height.

When moving on even terrains, it automatically adjusts into wheeled mode to maintain high efficiency. On discrete and uneven terrains, it switches to legged mode for superior stability.

The robot's movement gait adopts the diagonal gait pattern to simulate human walking, with two servo motors enabling omnidirectional movement. Each wheel-leg module includes fixed parts, servo motors, and connectors for robust performance.

Wheel-leg hybrid mechanism demonstrating adaptive locomotion

Biomimetic design inspired by natural wave patterns

2. Novel Biomimetic Wave-Shaped Wheel

By combining a smooth surface with a wave-like concave-convex profile, our proprietary wheel design offers advantages in adaptability to complex terrains, motion stability and energy efficiency.

It not only absorbs the advantages of four-legged support but also enhances mobility through the wave-shaped tread, meeting the requirements of lunar exploration operations. The wave-shaped tread does not require a complex track structure and has a more compact volume.

Moreover, the wave-shaped tread is less likely to get tangled with weeds, ropes and other debris, resulting in lower maintenance costs and enabling the robot to continuously and stably perform its functions during long-term and high-intensity rescue and exploration tasks.

3. Camera Image Transmission & Automatic Obstacle Avoidance

Based on exploration requirements, the robot is equipped with 360° imaging capability and can switch between legged and wheeled locomotion modes in real time according to terrain features identified through visual feedback.

To address space communication constraints, we have integrated a gait control module directly into the robot's control board, enabling remote operation and immediate gait switching, thereby reducing the negative impact of signal latency on operational efficiency.

The robot features automatic obstacle avoidance for obstacles up to 10 cm in length, the ability to traverse obstacles up to 30 cm, a maximum speed of 100 cm/s on flat terrain, and high-performance mobility on sloped surfaces.

360° imaging system for comprehensive environmental perception

Key Functions

Advanced capabilities enabling superior performance in extreme environments.

Transformable Wheel-Leg Dual Gait

Diagonal wheel-leg control achieves an efficient balance of speed and stability with excellent endurance performance. The diagonal gait can flexibly handle rugged environments such as gravel and shallow pits, leveraging evenly distributed support points to real-time adjust the robot's posture.

Automatic Obstacle Avoidance

Integrates a camera and ultrasonic sensing system to complete obstacle detection and avoidance without human intervention. When ultrasonic sensors detect an obstacle 10 cm ahead, all movements immediately stop and return to initial position.

Image Recognition & Transmission

The camera is equipped with a rotating motor, supporting 360° full-angle image collection and transmission for comprehensive environmental perception. Image data is real-time transmitted to the control terminal.

Simple Operation

No complex professional operation required; preset commands can be executed with just a click. Core action groups are pre-written into the control board, allowing quick switching between movement modes based on terrain images.

Validation Experiments

Comprehensive testing validating the feasibility and performance of our innovations.

1. Gait Testing Experiment

The robot body was assembled with wheel-leg modules and connected to the control board. Upper-computer parameters were adjusted to debug both forward and backward gaits.

Eight sets of parameters were used to test forward motion, followed by another eight sets for a six-step backward gait. The experiment verified the feasibility of practical parameter tuning for the proposed wheel-leg hybrid gait.

2. Complex Terrain Crossing Experiment

Testing the robot's ability to traverse challenging landscapes including slopes over 25° and obstacles exceeding 20 cm in height.

The hybrid wheel-leg system demonstrated 50% improvement in obstacle clearance capability compared to traditional wheeled systems.

Video Demonstration:

Crossing Complex Terrain Experiment - Demonstrating the robot's ability to overcome steep slopes and obstacles

3. Obstacle Avoidance Experiment

A camera was connected to the mobile control application. Four target obstacles were captured, and all images transmitted were clear and stable.

The obstacle-avoidance threshold was set to 10 cm. The robot successfully stopped within the 10 cm threshold in 80% of trials, while the remaining cases exceeded the threshold by only 0.5-1 cm.

Video Demonstration:

Obstacle Avoidance Experiment - Demonstrating automatic obstacle detection and avoidance functionality

4. Load Testing Experiment

Testing the robot's payload capacity and stability under loaded conditions. The robot demonstrated reliable performance while carrying scientific instruments and samples.

Performance was evaluated in weeds, debris, and soil. Traditional Mecanum wheels exhibited a jamming probability of up to 40% due to debris adhesion.

In contrast, the biomimetic wave-shaped wheels maintained stable operation in all three environments, with a debris adhesion rate of only 4%-5% and no significant impact on locomotion.

To date, the team has completed all four experiments, achieved stable dual-gait debugging, implemented image-based obstacle avoidance, completed wave-shaped wheel testing, and validated payload capacity for practical applications.

Our Mission

Our team was formed by a shared passion for aerospace and robotics, nurtured through years of reading related books and attending industry exhibitions. This deep interest drove us to compete in the Conrad Challenge's Aerospace & Aviation track.

We were motivated by a critical flaw in current planetary exploration: the high risk of rover rollovers on extreme terrains, which can lead to catastrophic mission failure and immense financial loss. To solve this fundamental issue of stability and robustness, we innovated a quadrupedal wheel-leg robot.

This hybrid design merges the speed of wheels on flat ground with the superior stability and adaptability of legs on rough landscapes, ensuring mission continuity where traditional rovers would fail.
